The arc

It started with numbers, not models.

I started in accounting and finance, in the rooms where the numbers are owed an explanation. Reconciliations, audits, the slow craft of getting a story to match its evidence.

From there I moved onto outdoor power equipment dealership floors as a data scientist — Dealer Management System (DMS) exports, inventory drift, sales pacing. Spreadsheets that ran the business and somehow ran the people too. I learned that data is only worth what the operator does with it the next morning.

Then the agents arrived, and the same instinct came with them. Build the system that reads the field, weighs the evidence, and gives the person on the floor one good move.
